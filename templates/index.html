Home

Welcome to our database-driven website! Here, you can create and manipulate a database representation of a multi-city power grid. The basis of this database system is to manage and track the energy demands of cities and the supply of energy to them via entities such as power sources, substations, local generators, and more. To get started with demonstrative sample data, click the 'Load Sample Data' button below. If you want to start from scratch, feel free to click 'Clear Database'! If you're curious about the details of a table in the database, visit the page for that database using the nav bar above to see a more specific description. To create, read, update, or delete data from a table, visit the page associated with that table. You can update a row in the table by directly interacting with and modifying the contents of a cell in the table, and then click the 'Update' button. Similarly, you can delete a row in a table by clicking the 'Delete' button on the right side of the row. At the bottom of the page there is a form for creating a new row in the table, simply fill it out and click the 'Add ...' button below it.

Note: While our focus this term remained on building the fundamental structure of our database and accompanying website, some features such as the automatic calculation of the current loads through entities or demand scaling with the 'consumption policy' field of City HQs is not yet implemented. We do plan to implement additional UX features such as these after the term concludes, but for now some fields must be calculated manually if accuracy is desired.
